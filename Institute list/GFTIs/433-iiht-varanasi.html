About the Institute

Background of Institute

In olden days Indian Handloom Industry was not having trained technical personnel and was solely dependent on the expertise and knowledge passed on from generation to generation for its survival and development. It was in this context that the Government of India decided to set up Indian Institutes of Handloom Technology with the prime objective of providing technical personnel trained in new methods and latest development in the field of Handloom to enable the Handloom industry to face the challenges from other sector of the Textile Industry. The first Indian Institute of Handloom Technology was established in 1956 at Varanasi by taking over the Central Weaving Institute, Varanasi. The second Institute was established in 1960 at Salem in Tamil Nadu by taking over the Government Textile Institute at Madras and shifting the same to Salem, one of the important Handloom Weaving Centers in southern India. At present there are six Indian Institutes of Handloom Technology in the Central Sector and four Institutes in State Sector.

The Central Sector Institutes are located at

·         Varanasi in Uttar Pradesh (Estd. In 1956)

·         Salem in Tamil Nadu (Estd. In 1960)

·         Guwahati in Assam (Estd. In 1982)

·         Jodhpur in Rajasthan (Estd. In 1993)

·         Bargarh in Orissa (Estd. In 2008).

·         Shantipur in West Bengal (Estd. In 2015)

The State Sector Institutes are located at

·         Venkatagiri in Andhra Pradesh

·         Gadag in Karnataka.

·         Champa in Chhattisgarh

·         Kannur in Kerala.

 

 

 

About the Institute

The Govt. of India, being impressed with the great potential that existed for developing the Central Weaving Institute both as major research hub as well as training Institute for providing trained man-power to the Handloom sector in the field of Handloom Technology, decided to take over this Institute. During the year 1956. Accordingly, on 17th. Sept., 1956, the Central Weaving Institute Was taken over by All India Handloom Board on behalf of the Central Govt. of India and thus became a part of the Textile Commissioner’s Office and the Institute was renamed as Indian Institute of Handloom Technology subsequently after setting of O/o DC (Handloom) this institute has brought under the aegis of O/o Development Commissioner for Handlooms, M/o Textiles.

·         In 1962, the re-organized long term (3 years) programme, viz. Diploma in Handloom Technology was started which has been re-named as Diploma in Handloom & Textile Technology since academic session 2009-10. The institute has switched over to SEMESTER System effective from the Academic Session, i.e., 2011-12. Apart from DHTT, a Post Diploma programme in Textile Chemistry has been introduced in 1983, with three semester duration which is renamed as Post Diploma in Textile Processing w.e.f. 2009-2010.

_·         _A new UG programme B.Tech Handloom & Textile Technology has been started in this institution from the academic year 2021-22 onwards. The programme is approved by AICTE, New Delhi and affiliated to Dr. A.P.J. Abdual Kalam Technical University, Lucknow_. This B.Tech Handloom & Textile Technology is a unique programme which is offered only by this institution in North India.

_·         _This programme has another unique feature of offering the stipend of Rs. 3000/- Per Month for the students during the study period.

Student Life at Institute

Students Life at Campus

The Institute has a strong Counselling Service to help students settle down comfortably in the new environment and cope with the stresses of student life. Sincere and dedicated student volunteers, helped by faculty advisers, organize the orientation programme for the fresh entrants, provide extra help in course work, if needed, and conduct language classes, etc. The students are involved in all the decision making processes of the Institute via student representatives in various Institute committees. The Institute has a fully residential picturesque campus, spread over 8.3 acres of land, with all modern amenities. It has about 180 Diploma, 30 post diploma students, and 20 B.Tech students, around 18 faculty members and 50 supporting staff. Each hostel has a mess, providing breakfast, lunch, evening tea and dinner to all residents during designated hours. The students are accommodated in two boys’ hostels and one girls’ hostels. All rooms in the hostels have a provision for internet connectivity and connection to other computers through Wi-Fi Network.

 The Health assistant provides round-the-clock medical help and emergency care to the campus community.

Location and Accessibility

 

Location

      

The Institute is located in Holy City Varanasi. The campus of IIHT is about approx. 2 Kms from Varanasi Junction Railway Station (BSB) located at the heart of Varanasi city and is free from pollution providing a peaceful atmosphere for studies and research. The campus is spread over 8.3 acres of land comprising of  building with space for Administration, Classrooms, Laboratories including Computer lab with own internet facility, Seminar Hall, Auditorium , Board Room, Library & Digital Library and Workshop. The campus is equipped with facilities like Residential accommodation for employees, Water supply and Generator system with 24 hours backup.  

 

Arrival by AIR: It is recommended that visitors fly into the Lal Bahudar Shastri International Airport, Babatpur, Varanasi located about 20 Kms from IIHT Varanasi. You can hire taxis at the airport. It takes about 45 minutes to 1 hour to reach at IIHT, campus.

 

Arrival by TRAIN: Varanasi junction is well connected to most cities in North, East, South, West and Central India. It is located on the Delhi-Kolkata train route. IIHT Varanasi campus is located at a distance of about 02 kilometres from the Varanasi junction and about 06 Kms from Banaras station.
